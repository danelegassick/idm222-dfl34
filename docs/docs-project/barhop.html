BarHop

This was a freelance project that a previous professor reached out to me for. This was my first freelance project. I was given general guidelines for what this project would include, but given no brand guidelines for this, so I could develop something from scratch.

UX/UI

Freelance

App Design

Barhop is a developing business that is oriented at optimizing the night out for bar hoppers, providing data on local bars and promoting events for locals to attend. I was tasked with designing the homepage for an upcoming mobile app.

BRAND IDENTITY

As the brand is associated with young people going out to barhop, I thought the colors should be fun: a beer-ish yellow as the primary color, with some green, blue, and purple accents to capture the essence of a party. The font was a simple sans-serif, as there wasn't much text on the screen, but the icons captured a bit more personality. Dark-mode was chosen, as this app would be used more frequently at night.

MANAGING DATA

As the app has a lot of data to reveal to users, I had planned originally to give it a more tech dashboard look. However, after mocking up some designs and consulting with the client, this wouldn't work. The app needed to be friendly.

REASSESSING DECISIONS

With a more friendly look in mind, some changes had to be made. My goals: more images, more icons, blockier uses of color. Seen below are the elements that needed to change.

RAPID SKETCHING

To find a combination of images, icons, and colors that worked, I rapid fired some sketches out and gathered the best ideas in order to come up a friendlier interface.

NEW FEATURES

For this project, I was also not told all of the features, so I could build out what I felt would be useful. Some of the ideas I prioritized were a favorites section, events section, map section, and featured section. The favorites section didn't make it into the final design (as per client request), but the others were well recieved.
